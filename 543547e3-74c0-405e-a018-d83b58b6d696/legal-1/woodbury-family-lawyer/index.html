Our Main Practice Areas of Family Law in Woodbury, TN

The state of Tennessee sees an overall divorce rate of 2.9 cases per every 1,000 residents, and a divorce rate of 9.1 per every female member of the population. While divorce is often the most common practice area of family law, at Boss Law, we are also here to help our clients through the following legal issues:

  • Child custody: When a divorce involves children, the parents will need to determine how custody of these children is arranged in the event of a divorce, paternity case, or legal separation. If the parents are unable to reach an agreement on custody on their own, they will need to look to a family court judge to make this decision for them. The judge will take certain factors into account to determine the best interests of the child, including:
    • The child's relationship with each parent, their siblings, and other relatives, including grandparents
    • The affection, love, and emotional ties between the child and each of their parents
    • The willingness of both parents to work together in order to support their child's well-being
    • If there is any history of substance abuse or domestic violence on the part of either parent
    • If the child is of a certain age and maturity level, the preferences of that child
    • The stability of each parent's income and their home life
  • Child support: Following a divorce, paternity action, or legal separation, one parent may be required to pay child support to the other parent. In 2021 alone, 4.1 million parents across the United States were receiving child support payments in the form of cash. In TN, both parents are held responsible for caring for the financial needs of their children. Typically, the noncustodial parent will be required to offer support to the other.
  • Spousal support: In Tennessee, spousal support, also known as alimony, is a legal obligation for the higher-earning spouse to offer financial support to the lower-earning spouse in cases of divorce. Alimony is not involved in every divorce case, however, and there are certain factors that the courts will consider when determining support awards, duration, and amounts, including:
    • The length of the marriage
    • The contributions of each spouse, including staying home and taking care of the household and children
    • The mental and physical health of the lower-earning spouse
    • The standard of living that was established during the marriage
  • Property division: In a divorce, Tennessee operates under the law of equitable distribution, meaning that all marital property will be divided equally between the couple, but not necessarily in a perfect 50/50 manner. Marital property includes any assets or debts obtained during the marriage, such as the family home, vehicles, mortgage loans, personal items, business interests, retirement accounts, and credit card debt.
  • High net worth divorces: In cases of high-net-worth individuals seeking divorce, the issues of normal divorce cases become all the more heightened. Higher spousal support or child support amounts may be pursued, and the division of marital property may become more complicated, especially when highly valuable assets are taken into consideration, such as multiple real estate properties or substantial collections.
  • Business owner's divorce: When a business is owned by either one spouse or both and a divorce occurs, it is understandable that you would want to protect your business. A divorce has the potential to harm your business in a variety of ways, and an attorney can help protect both the business and your interests.
  • Adoption: In Tennessee, anyone above the age of minority has the ability to adopt another individual. Adoption can be a tricky legal issue, however, and the knowledge and experience of a family lawyer is crucial to the success of your adoption.
  • Paternity: Paternity actions are difficult cases. Whether you are the child, mother, or alleged father of a child, you need to hire a family lawyer who can help you through the complications and necessary steps of seeking paternity. Maybe you are the supposed father looking to gain custody, or the mother hoping to obtain financial support. Either way, we are here to serve you.
