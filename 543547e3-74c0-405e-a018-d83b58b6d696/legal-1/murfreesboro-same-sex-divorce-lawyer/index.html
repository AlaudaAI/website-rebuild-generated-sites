Issues Unique to Same Sex Divorces in Murfreesboro, TN

The state of Tennessee sees a same sex marriage rate of 4.38 per every 1,000 households, creating a total of around 10,900 same sex couples. These relationships were not recognized as legal marriages until 2015, so the laws surrounding them are still relatively new. While most same sex marriages have many of the same complications as heterosexual marriages, there are some challenges that are unique to LGBTQ unions. These challenges include:

  • Child custody: According to one study, around 18% of all LGBTQ people are parenting children under the age of 18. When a marriage between a same sex couple breaks down, they will need to determine arrangements regarding custody of their minor children. If a child has been adopted by both parents, then those individuals likely have legal parental rights to the child, but in cases of surrogacy or IVF, custody can become complicated.
  • Property division: Because same sex marriages are still relatively new in Murfreesboro, TN, many couples have been cohabitating for longer than heterosexual couples. During cohabitation, the partners will accumulate certain assets and even build wealth together. When divorce occurs, these assets will need to be divided between the two spouses. Longer periods of cohabitation can make this a more difficult process.
  • Spousal support: Similar to the cohabitation issues of property division in a same sex divorce, the award of spousal support can be based on how long the marriage lasted. This can cause an extra layer of difficulty for same sex couples who have perhaps been living together for the last 30 years, but only married for the last 10.
  • Distrust of the justice system: When you take a look at history, the American justice system has not always proven itself as trustworthy to members of the LGBTQ community. When it comes to a same sex divorce, either party may feel concern regarding bias and how the judge will treat them.
