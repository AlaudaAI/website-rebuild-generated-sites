    How is BHRT administered?

    Bioidentical Hormone Replacement Therapy (BHRT) can be administered in several forms, depending on your unique needs and preferences. Common delivery methods include creams, which are applied topically for slow absorption, pellets that are inserted under the skin for consistent hormone release, and injections that deliver precise doses quickly.

    Each method has its own benefits and is selected after evaluating your lifestyle, medical history, and treatment goals. For example, pellets provide long-term convenience, while creams allow for daily adjustments. Our team at Truly Lifted MedSpa will work with you to determine the best administration method for your personalized plan, ensuring safety, comfort, and effective results.

    How soon will I see results?

    Most patients start noticing improvements within 2 to 4 weeks after beginning BHRT, though individual experiences can vary. Early changes often include better sleep quality, increased energy, and enhanced mental clarity. Over time, additional benefits like improved mood, stabilized weight, and enhanced libido become more apparent.

    It's important to note that BHRT works gradually as your body adjusts to optimized hormone levels, so continued improvements may be seen over several months. Regular follow-ups are essential to monitor your progress and fine-tune your treatment plan for the best results. Patience and adherence to your therapy are key to achieving long-lasting benefits.

    Is BHRT safe?

    When administered by qualified professionals, Bioidentical Hormone Replacement Therapy is a safe and effective solution for addressing hormonal imbalances. BHRT uses hormones that are structurally identical to those naturally produced by your body, reducing the risks associated with synthetic alternatives.

    At Truly Lifted MedSpa, we ensure safety through comprehensive hormone testing, personalized treatment plans, and close monitoring throughout your therapy. Potential side effects, such as temporary mood shifts or slight swelling, are rare and manageable with proper care. Regular follow-ups allow us to adjust your therapy as needed, ensuring the treatment remains safe, effective, and tailored to your evolving health needs.
