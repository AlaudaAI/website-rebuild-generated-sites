Benefits of a Chemical Peel Treatment

Chemical peels can help address a wide range of skin issues, from acne scars and hyperpigmentation to fine lines and wrinkles. They stimulate the production of collagen, promoting cellular regeneration and unveiling a fresher, younger-looking complexion.

Types of Chemical Peels

Chemical peels come in various strengths, allowing them to be tailored to your specific skin concerns. These treatments include:

  • Superficial peels: Using mild acids like alpha-hydroxy acid, superficial peels gently exfoliate the skin's outer layer, improving its texture and tone.
  • Medium peels: Using stronger substances such as trichloroacetic acid, medium peels penetrate deeper to correct moderate skin damage and aging signs.
  • Deep peels: These peels, using phenol, provide the most dramatic results, addressing severe wrinkles, sun damage, or scarring.

Suitable Candidates

Chemical peels are an excellent choice for those who want to improve the appearance and feel of their skin. You may be a good candidate if:

  • You are in good overall health.
  • You want to address issues like acne scars, hyperpigmentation, or signs of aging.
  • You have realistic expectations for the treatment.
  • You do not have any specific contraindications, such as severe skin conditions, infections, or allergies to the peel components.

What to Expect During & After the Procedure

A chemical peel treatment is a straightforward procedure that typically lasts from 30 minutes to an hour.

During the Procedure

The solution is applied to your skin, where it remains for a set period. You may feel a tingling or warm sensation as the peel works.

After the procedure

Depending on the peel's intensity, your skin may be red and slightly swollen, and you may experience peeling over the following days as the old skin sheds to reveal the new.

Recovery Time

Recovery time depends on the type of peel; superficial peels require little to no downtime, while medium to deep peels may require one to two weeks for skin to heal completely.

Do's and Don'ts Before & After the Procedure

To maximize the benefits from your chemical peel, follow these recommendations:

Do's:

  • Consult with a skincare professional to determine the most suitable type of peel for you.
  • Keep your skin hydrated and avoid sun exposure before the treatment.
  • Follow your provider's post-treatment care instructions, including using sunscreen and moisturizers.

Don't:

  • Don't use retinol or other strong skincare products in the days before the peel.
  • Don't pick at or forcefully remove peeling skin after the treatment; allow it to shed naturally.
  • Don't expose freshly peeled skin to the sun, as it will be more sensitive.
