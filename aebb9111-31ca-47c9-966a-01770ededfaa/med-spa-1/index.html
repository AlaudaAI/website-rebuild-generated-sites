003 — laser & ipl technology

Laser and Intense Pulsed Light (IPL) treatments are advanced skin rejuvenation techniques that target various concerns, including pigmentation, texture, and overall skin tone. These non-invasive treatments use light energy to stimulate collagen production, remove unwanted pigmentation, and improve skin clarity with minimal downtime.

Common uses of Laser & IPL in aesthetics include: Skin Rejuvenation: Improves texture, reduces fine lines, and boosts collagen. Hyperpigmentation & Sun Damage: Fades dark spots, melasma, and sun-induced discoloration. Rosacea & Redness Reduction: Minimizes broken capillaries and facial redness. Acne & Acne Scars: Targets active acne and improves the appearance of scarring. Hair Removal: Provides long-term reduction of unwanted hair on the face and body. Vascular Lesions & Spider Veins: Effectively treats small blood vessels and broken capillaries. Hand Rejuvenation: Restores a youthful appearance by evening out skin tone and reducing age spots.

Laser and IPL treatments offer a safe, customizable solution for achieving clear, youthful skin with long-lasting results.

004 — silhouette instalift®

Silhouette InstaLift® is a minimally invasive procedure designed to reposition sagging facial tissue and support the skin. It uses absorbable sutures and small bidirectional cones that create an immediate lifting effect while also stimulating collagen production over time.

The sutures are placed beneath the skin in targeted areas of the face. Each suture contains a series of tiny cones that hold the tissue in an elevated position. Over time, both the sutures and cones gradually dissolve and are safely absorbed by the body.

Threads (sutures): Made of poly-glycolide/L-lactide (PLGA), a well-studied biodegradable material commonly used in medical devices. Cones: Made of poly-L-lactic acid (PLLA), the same biocompatible material found in certain injectable collagen stimulators.

As the material dissolves, it stimulates the body's natural collagen production, which helps improve skin firmness and structure.

005 — what to expect

The procedure is performed in the office with local anesthesia. Most patients resume normal activities within 1–2 days. Temporary swelling, tenderness, or bruising may occur and typically resolves within a week.

Results: Immediate lifting effect. Continued improvement over several months as collagen develops. Results usually last 18–24 months.
