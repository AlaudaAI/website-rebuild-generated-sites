Our 7-Step Process

Step 1 : Meet

Regardless of your level of knowledge in financial matters, we provide financial counseling from our initial meeting through to our final follow-up session. The services we provide are the "missing link" in the divorce process.

If the financial issues of a divorce are left unaddressed, or, as is more often the case, attended to incompletely, it spells trouble for one or both ex-spouses down the road. Arons & Associates offers a unique and invaluable service to those divorcing, especially helpful for the less financially savvy spouse.

Joel Cherry, Attorney—San Francisco, CA

Step 2 : Discover

Our efficient process gathers your financial information. When this step is complete, you and your spouse will know the assets, debts, income and expenses for each of you. We'll use this information to help you avoid common money mistakes during a TN divorce — divorce traps and pitfalls that could lead to future financial troubles.

Sandy and our staff will examine financial details frequently overlooked during the divorce process including depreciation of assets and the effect of inflation on potential settlements. We'll provide thorough financial discovery and educate both spouses on the financial details. This is missing in typical divorces and mediation.

Step 3 : Analyze

Sandy and our staff will translate complex financial data into a clear picture of your marital estate. We use personalized reports to project your net worth and long-term cash flow needs after the divorce. Finally, we will create scenarios of property division, alimony, and child support for you and your spouse.

Need Sherlock Holmes?

Is the analysis simply not making sense? Do you think that your spouse is concealing income or inappropriate conduct? Are you aware of changes in your spouse's behavior, increased secrecy, illicit computer usage, or obsessive grooming habits? If so, we can refer you to associates who specialize in forensic accounting and can review financial documents and tax returns for evidence of:

  • Hidden assets
  • Salary deferrals
  • Excessive spending of marital funds while the TN marriage is dissolving
  • Transfer of cash or TN property to others
  • Acquiring or paying off large debts without your knowledge

Step 4 : Problem Solve

Creative problem solving is required when crafting settlements that meet your objectives. We can propose different scenarios for the division of assets / debt and create net worth projections for those options. Refer to our case study for more information. You'll understand what your short- and long-term financial future could resemble if you chose settlement option "A" versus "B". Accepting a divorce settlement does not have to resemble a game of "Pin the Tail on the Donkey".

Step 5 : Review

Our review sessions educate and prepare you to make smart financial decisions. We meet with you to explain the findings in our financial reports and answer questions in language you can understand. Our clients and their attorneys find review sessions to be particularly helpful before divorce mediation or court appearances.

Step 6 : Participate

Arons & Associates is an active participant as we guide you through the financial issues of your divorce. We frequently accompany clients and their attorneys at their divorce mediation sessions to provide on-the-spot analysis of proposed settlements. Our participation ensures clients and their divorce attorneys understand the financial ramifications and tax implications before agreeing to any proposed divorce settlement. We will also appear in court, if necessary, to explain our financial analysis.

Divorce does not have to be a battle. We can demonstrate ways to reduce conflict and stress for both spouses and your children. Reducing conflict during divorce encourages a better working relationship post-divorce.
