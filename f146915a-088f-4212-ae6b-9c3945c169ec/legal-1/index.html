About Freeman & Fuson

What you can expect when you work with us

Personal attention from your attorney, clear communication, and strategy built for results—not surprises.

Founded by Mark T. Freeman, Esq. and Joseph W. Fuson, Esq., Freeman & Fuson Law has built its reputation on being both formidable in the courtroom and compassionate with clients. Our attorneys are seasoned trial lawyers who prepare each case as if it will go before a judge and jury—because that preparation often leads to better outcomes in negotiations, too.

We believe legal representation should be as accessible as it is effective. That means clear communication from day one, honest expectations about costs and timelines, and a commitment to treating every case—no matter the size—with the attention it deserves. Our team has successfully handled matters ranging from high‑stakes criminal defense to sensitive family law disputes, complex civil litigation, and life‑altering injury claims.

The "Miranda warning" is a statement given by law enforcement to a suspect in custody, informing them of their rights. The warning is derived from the United States Supreme Court case, Miranda v. Arizona, 384 U.S. 436 (1966), which established the requirement for law enforcement to inform suspects of their rights before interrogating them...
