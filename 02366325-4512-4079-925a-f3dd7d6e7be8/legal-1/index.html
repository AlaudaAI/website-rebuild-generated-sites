10 Common Money Mistakes in TN Divorces

Mistake 01

Underestimating your expenses

You should make a complete list of your monthly living expenses, which includes cost-of-living increases for the duration of alimony. If you don't consider cost-of-living increases, your alimony probably won't cover your expenses after the first year.

Mistake 02

Assuming your attorney will handle everything

Your attorney is trained to handle legal issues. Even the best Nashville divorce attorneys are not certified divorce financial mediators and were not trained to perform financial analysis or address tax issues.

Mistake 03

Not correctly utilizing experts

Make sure that whoever's clock is ticking is the professional whose skills can be most advantageous to you at that point. Your result will be both better and more cost-effective.

Mistake 04

Not taking tax deductions

Did you know that divorce attorneys' fees for obtaining alimony and retirement funds may be tax-deductible in TN? We educate clients about the tax issues of divorce.

Mistake 05

Failing to communicate directly with your spouse

Two attorneys' fees combined can easily exceed $900 per hour. Save the extra cash for a vacation or IRA contribution. Discuss minor issues directly with your spouse.

Mistake 06

Concealing financial information

Don't do it! Time and money will be spent gathering information. If you fail to provide requested information, an attorney will subpoena documents. Assets will be found - it just takes more time and money.

Mistake 07

Not insuring alimony and child support

In most cases, alimony and child support stop upon death of the payor. Protect your stream of income by ensuring the settlement requires your spouse to maintain life insurance throughout the duration.

Mistake 08

Should the wife keep the house?

Can she really afford the mortgage, monthly maintenance, repairs, insurance, taxes, and utilities? If you forgo retirement savings to keep the house, you are gambling with your future.

Mistake 09

Not considering mediation

According to TN courts, 95% of all divorce cases are settled outside of court. Using a divorce financial mediator makes sense and is less expensive and stressful than litigation.

Mistake 10

Accepting an offer just to get it over with

During divorce, you will be making the most important financial decisions of your life. Take the time to do it right. Speak to a financial professional to understand the impacts of your potential settlement.
