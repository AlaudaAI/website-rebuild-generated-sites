A 50/50 Split

Tennessee is an "equitable distribution" state. That doesn't mean 50/50. It means fair, but fair to whom? You need to know the financial value of marital property to make an informed choice about the proposed settlement package.

Confused?

Many divorcees have a false sense of confidence because they don't understand what they don't know. Don't make a critical financial error because you couldn't see the forest for the trees. Arons & Associates offers divorce financial mediation services that will help clarify the important issues so that you can confidently make informed financial decisions during your divorce.

10 Common Money Mistakes in TN Divorces

  • Not understanding your household cash flow and spending before agreeing to spousal support
  • Not knowing what your marital assets and debts are worth
  • Giving up the house to keep other assets of supposedly equal value
  • Taking a share of your ex-spouse's 401(k) without knowing the tax implications
  • Not knowing what it costs to maintain the assets you're receiving
  • Agreeing to pay/receive spousal support without knowing your future tax obligations
  • Not considering what happens to spousal support if your ex-spouse dies or becomes disabled
  • Signing an agreement before knowing how much money you'll need to live on
  • Not knowing the real cost of providing health insurance for your children
  • Forgetting to change the beneficiaries on your life insurance policies, 401(k)s, and IRAs after your divorce is final
