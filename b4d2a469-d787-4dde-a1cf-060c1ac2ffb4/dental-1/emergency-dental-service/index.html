When to Seek Emergency Dental Care

Dental emergencies can happen at any time and require immediate attention. If you experience any of the following, contact our emergency dental service:

  • Severe tooth pain or toothache
  • Knocked-out or broken teeth
  • Lost dental fillings or crowns
  • Swollen or bleeding gums
  • Dental abscess or infection
  • Jaw injury or trauma
  • Objects stuck between teeth

What to Do in a Dental Emergency

While waiting for your emergency appointment, here are some immediate steps you can take:

For Severe Pain

Rinse your mouth with warm salt water and take over-the-counter pain medication as directed. Apply a cold compress to the outside of your cheek if there is swelling.

For a Knocked-Out Tooth

Handle the tooth by the crown (not the root), rinse gently with water if dirty, and try to place it back in the socket. If that's not possible, keep it moist in milk or saliva until you can see a dentist.

For a Broken Tooth

Save any pieces of the broken tooth, rinse your mouth with warm water, and apply a cold compress to reduce swelling.
