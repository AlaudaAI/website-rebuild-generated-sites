About Us

Experienced Divorce Attorneys & Counsellors in Tennessee

Let Our Family Help Yours

At Hammond Zettersten & Associates, we handle sensitive family law matters for clients in Williamson, Maury, and Rutherford Counties and throughout the surrounding middle Tennessee area. Started as a husband and wife legal team, our attorneys understand families. They have helped numerous clients overcome the most critical legal matters anyone can face — those that affect the family. Christina Hammond Zettersten and her associates are accomplished lawyers, counselors, and advocates. They work with clients personally to help them navigate complex and emotional issues ranging from divorce and property division to child custody and adoption.

Christina started this practice with her husband, Peter, who is now working for the State of Tennessee. Christina now works with her associate, Leslea, and Of Counsel, Jimmy, and they have offices in Brentwood and Murfreesboro. Christina additionally works in child support enforcement for the 21st and 32nd Judicial Districts. Christina is admitted to the Tennessee Bar and is a member of the Tennessee Bar Association . She is also admitted to practice before the United States District Court for the Middle District of Tennessee. She was previously admitted to the Kansas and Minnesota Bars prior to relocating to the Nashville area. Christina provides legal services in Williamson, Rutherford, Maury, and surrounding counties, focusing on family law.

Christina graduated from Washburn University School of Law in Topeka, Kansas where she earned a certificate in law and government. During law school Christina participated in the law clinic where she represented clients in divorce, adoption, and immigration cases. During law school, she studied abroad at the University of the West Indies in Barbados. She also interned at the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces in Washington, D.C. Prior to moving to the Nashville area, Christina worked for the State of Kansas in the area of administrative law. In her free time, Christina enjoys running, cycling, and spending time with her family.

J. Jimmy Leo Richardson, III. Esq.

Of Counsel

Phone: (615) 896-5720
Fax: (615) 468-4688
Email: jimmy@zetterstenlaw.com

J. Jimmy Leo Richardson III serves as Of Counsel with Hammond Zettersten & Associates, bringing over a decade of courtroom experience and a deep-rooted commitment to child advocacy and public service.

Jimmy is a seasoned juvenile and family law attorney known for his courtroom presence, work against the Department of Children's Services, and passionate representation of Tennessee's most vulnerable children often serving as Guardian ad Litem. In addition to practicing across Middle Tennessee, Jimmy has served as an attorney in the community in a variety of areas: Criminal Jury Trials, Guardian ad Litem Representation for abused or neglected children, court-appointed juvenile delinquent cases, dependency and neglect matters, termination of parental rights, and conservatorship proceedings and Adoptions.

Before joining the firm, Jimmy operated his own private practice, where he developed a reputation for tenacity, moral clarity, and heartfelt advocacy. He has briefed and argued matters before the Tennessee Court of Appeals and is frequently appointed in high-conflict or emergency matters.

Jimmy's commitment to justice extends beyond the courtroom. He served a full term on the Murfreesboro City School Board before becoming the Vice Chairman of the Board in his second Term and has held multiple public service appointments, including the Rutherford County Beer Board, the Town of Smyrna Historic Zoning Commission, and the Smyrna Charity Assistance Board.

He and his wife, Shaina Blair Richardson, a licensed clinical social worker, live in Murfreesboro with their two daughters. The family attends Saint Rose of Lima Catholic Church.

Your Ally in Troubling Times

We know that divorce, child custody, and other family law issues come with profound emotional currents. While feelings of anxiety, insecurity, pain, loss, and fear are common, so is love. These complex, interrelated emotions are just one of the challenges facing you over the future of your family. While we cannot eliminate the stress and anguish of divorce, we can provide you with an understanding ear and a clear-minded perspective on these matters. You may face trying times, but you don't have to go through them alone. We are here to guide you through all aspects of the legal process, protecting your rights so you can look ahead to a brighter tomorrow.
