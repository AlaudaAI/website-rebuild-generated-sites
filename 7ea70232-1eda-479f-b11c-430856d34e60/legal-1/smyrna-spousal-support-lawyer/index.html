What Is Spousal Support?

Spousal support is a court-ordered payment that one spouse provides to the other following a divorce. Oftentimes, one spouse makes more money than the other, and alimony is a way to make sure the receiving spouse can maintain their standard of living.

There are four main types of spousal support, which include the following:

  • Alimony in solido. This type of alimony is given over time in larger sums.
  • Rehabilitative alimony. This is short-term support meant to help a spouse become self-sufficient.
  • Reimbursement alimony. Conversely, reimbursement alimony serves to repay a spouse for money they spent on the other's education or job training.
  • Alimony in futuro. Also known as periodic alimony, this type of support lasts until the receiving partner remarries.

Who Can Receive Alimony?

Alimony is not guaranteed in divorce cases. First, a spouse must ask for it. The court will not take it upon itself to include alimony in the final divorce order. Additionally, a spouse must show that they need additional support after the marriage to either maintain their quality of life or prepare themselves for the job market. This is especially true if they did not work while they were married. Spouses can also request alimony in exchange for giving up certain assets.

Regardless of the reason for spousal support, it is rarely permanent. In many cases, these payments serve only to help the receiving spouse adjust to their new life. In other cases, payments cease when the recipient remarries or even moves in with a new partner.

FAQs

What Disqualifies You From Alimony in TN?

You can be disqualified from receiving alimony if you cannot demonstrate to the court that you have a genuine need for financial support or if the other spouse does not have the ability to pay support. If you are self-supporting or earn a similar income to your partner, it could be difficult to convince the court that you deserve spousal support. Additionally, you could lose your right to alimony if you engaged in fault-based misconduct.

Do I Need a Lawyer to File for Spousal Support?

No, you do not technically need a lawyer to file for spousal support, but it is unwise to go through the process on your own. Spousal support laws can be challenging to navigate, but an experienced lawyer can manage your alimony case and pursue what you deserve. An attorney has the legal knowledge and negotiation skills to effectively advocate for you.

How Is Spousal Support Determined in TN?

When determining spousal support, the court does not have a specific formula to follow. Rather, it assesses the reported needs of one spouse and compares that to the other's ability to pay. The court looks at each partner's income, employment, and future prospects to determine what's fair. Similarly, a judge will consider the length of the marriage and the cause for its breakdown.

Can Spousal Support Be Modified After a Divorce?

Yes, spousal support can be modified after a divorce. Spouses can request modifications to the divorce order if either party has experienced significant changes in their lives. If a paying spouse loses their job, they may apply to have payments reduced or terminated. Similarly, if the receiving spouse remarries or moves in with a new partner, alimony payments may end. Conversely, if a receiving spouse can show an increased need, they can file for an increase.
