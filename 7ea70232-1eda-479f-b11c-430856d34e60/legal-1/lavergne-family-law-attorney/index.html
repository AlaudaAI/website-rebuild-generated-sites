FAQs

What Is the Biggest Mistake in a Custody Battle?

The biggest mistake in a custody battle is letting your emotions get the better of you. You want to stick to the facts rather than resorting to inflammatory remarks about the other parent. Remember, the goal of a custody battle is to create an agreement that's in your child's best interest. Insulting the other parent, exaggerating to make them look bad, and telling lies to discredit them will only serve to work against you in the judge's eyes.

How Long Does a Divorce Take in Tennessee?

It's difficult to say how long a divorce takes. Ultimately, it depends on the complexity of the case and the cooperation of the spouses. If the case is simple and spouses are willing to work with each other, a divorce can be finalized in just a few weeks. If there are many assets to divide, custody issues, and hostile spouses, the case can take months or even years to resolve.

Can You Modify a Child or Spousal Support Order?

Yes, you can modify a child or spousal support order. You'll have to show a serious change in circumstances that warrants a modification, though. For child support, you may have to demonstrate that the child's needs have changed or that one of the parents has experienced a change in income. For spousal support, a change in their job or ability to page may justify a modification.

What Rights Do Fathers Have in Tennessee?

In Tennessee, fathers have the same rights as mothers. That means they have the same rights to custody, visitation, and decision-making. In custody battles, they can pursue joint or sole custody of the child with the same considerations as the mother does. However, unmarried fathers first must prove paternity to retain any legal rights over the child.
