Common Types of High-Value Assets

High asset divorces are typically defined as divorce cases where the couple owns at least one million dollars' worth of assets jointly. In Murfreesboro, where the average home sells for a median price of $435,000, it is not hard for couples to reach the definition of being regarded as high assets.

Tennessee operates under the rule of equitable distribution of marital property in a divorce. While this does not necessarily mean everything will be split in an even, 50/50 manner, the judge will try to ensure the split is fair and equitable to each spouse. In order to ensure that an equitable distribution does take place, the couple will need to review and document all of their shared assets, including:

  • Business assets: When a couple owns a business, or one of the spouses created a business during the marriage, these are considered to be marital assets and therefore, subject to the laws of equitable distribution. This can include all commercial property, company vehicles, equipment related to the business, related IP, and any office furniture or fixtures.
  • Retirement accounts: 83% of all adults who have an annual household income of at least $100,000 also have a retirement savings plan. Any IRA, 401(k), or other retirement account that was earned during the marriage is considered to be shared or marital property and subject to division. If a retirement account was set up prior to a marriage, it may be considered separate, unless any interests or gains were accumulated during the marriage.
  • Pensions: In Tennessee, any pension benefits that were earned during a marriage are considered to be shared assets and therefore are subject to division. If one of the parties earned large pension benefits prior to the marriage, the courts can compare the duration of the marriage to their years of employment in order to ensure equitable distribution takes place.
  • Investments: Investments made during the marriage or those that have gained value during the marriage are subject to division in a divorce. The courts will estimate the value of these investments by looking at both the possible future value after appreciation and the current value of what you hold. However, it is important to keep in mind that this valuation can be complicated when investments involve licenses or business shares.
  • Multiple homes or properties: If real estate is gained during marriage, these properties will be divided between the couple. Couples with high levels of wealth can typically accumulate properties such as a family home, vacation homes, timeshares, commercial properties, and rental properties, which generate an income.
  • Vehicle collections: When either spouse acquires cars, boats, or other vehicles during the marriage, these vehicles are subject to division. Tennessee is a popular state to collect highly valued vehicles, and during a divorce, all vehicles should be reviewed and catalogued appropriately so they can be evenly divided between the couple.
  • Other valuable collections: Individuals with high levels of wealth might also be collectors of fine artwork and jewelry. These items should all be valued during a divorce in order to ensure a fair and equitable distribution takes place.
  • Offshore accounts: If either spouse owns any offshore accounts, this can add a layer of difficulty to the case. International tax and property laws can present certain challenges that should only be handled by an experienced and trustworthy attorney.

Unique Considerations in a Murfreesboro, TN High-Asset Divorce

High asset divorce cases in Murfreesboro, TN, present several unique challenges. Couples with large amounts of wealth usually find themselves concerned about having to publicly disclose their financial information in a courtroom. Your attorney may be able to ask that the judge seal all financial records and ensure confidentiality is maintained.

There are also certain tax implications that can arise in a high-asset divorce. Certain tax consequences might be triggered if the sale, transfer, or property is not done in the correct manner. For these reasons, hiring an attorney to help ensure everything is done by the book and in a way that protects you is vital.
