What Assets Are Considered in a Tennessee Divorce Case?

In 2024, the average household income for residents of Woodbury was $58,466. The average home value was $216,400. These high-value assets need careful handling during divorce cases to make sure they stay protected.

During your divorce, you and your spouse can work together to discuss the division of your marital assets. Marital assets are anything acquired by either spouse during the duration of the marriage. This can include:

  • Income
  • Bank accounts and cash
  • Debt
  • Inheritances
  • Real estate and rental properties
  • Cryptocurrency
  • Investment accounts
  • Retirement accounts and IRAs
  • Businesses
  • Pets
  • Vehicles
  • Collections, such as high-value jewelry or watch collection
  • Other personal belongings

Property Division Laws During a Tennessee Divorce

Property gained before marriage is typically not split during divorce, unless the asset has become mixed with a marital asset. No one gets married expecting to divorce one day. It can be nearly impossible to keep your assets truly separate from your spouse during a marriage, especially during long-term marriages.

For example, if you gained an inheritance before getting married, but then used a portion of that money to make improvements to your marital home, that money spent becomes subject to division.

If you and your spouse can't agree on how to divide your property, the court will divide it equitably, not necessarily equally. For example, if you make a significant amount more in income than your spouse, and your spouse gave up a promotion in their career to help raise the children, they may be awarded the marital home post-divorce.

FAQs

How Can I Avoid Going to Court During My Divorce Case?

To avoid having to go to court during your divorce case, you and your attorney can work with your spouse and their legal counsel through mediation sessions to resolve your divorce issues. These sessions allow the couple to maintain control over the outcome of their case and are confidential, quick, and less expensive than traditional courts. However, if you are unable to negotiate during mediation, the case will proceed to trial, where a judge will decide for you.

How Is Alimony Determined During a Divorce in Tennessee?

To determine alimony during a divorce in Tennessee, the court will likely take many factors into consideration. Alimony, also called spousal support, is a financial payment made from one spouse to the other post-divorce. To decide on alimony, courts may consider the age and health of both spouses, their present and future earning potential, contributions and sacrifices each spouse made during the marriage, and the standard of living upheld during the marriage.

What Are the Different Types of Spousal Support in Tennessee?

There are several different types of spousal support that may be awarded in a Tennessee divorce case. Rehabilitative alimony is awarded to help the receiving spouse become financially independent after a period post-divorce. Long-term alimony may be awarded if the receiving spouse doesn't have the ability to become financially independent, such as having a disability or needing to raise several children. Transitional alimony is for a fixed period, and lump sum alimony is to avoid a payment plan.
